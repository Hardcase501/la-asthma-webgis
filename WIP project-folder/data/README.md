Project: Freeways & Communities — Los Angeles
Author: Ian (Master's Project)

Project description:
This project explores the relationships between proximity/exposure to freeway corridors and neighborhood-level outcomes in Los Angeles County, focusing on health, economic, and demographic indicators. Analyses will include spatial joins, buffer-based exposure metrics, and web-based visualizations.

Potential / planned datasets:
- Census tract or block-group shapefiles (U.S. Census Bureau) — will use ACS 5-year tables for demographics and income (CSV/GeoJSON/shapefile).
- LA County or CA state health outcome indicators (county or neighborhood-level CSV/GeoJSON), e.g., asthma prevalence, hospital admission rates.
- Traffic and freeway centerlines (shapefile or GeoJSON) — major arterials and interstate/freeway geometry for buffer analysis.
- CalEnviroScreen or environmental exposure indices (CSV/GeoJSON) — for pollution and cumulative impacts.
- Parcel or land-use layers (optional) — for finer-scale socioeconomic context.
- Point-level facilities (schools, hospitals, monitoring stations) — CSV/GeoJSON.
